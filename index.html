What's On
We Have Our Annual School Ochestral Performance
On 30 March Every Year

If you haven't seen it before or heard it for that matter you don't know what your missing out on! Every year the school ochestra puts on a open air performance and it is amazing it sounds awesome, evertyone is dressed in their best and the ochestra palys all night long. If you miss it you're actually missing out on a lot of fun and joy. If you get hungry we got you covered with an all you can eat buffet and a lot of cold soft drinks and water. In all it is the perfect ime to spend your night, listening to music and eating anything under the stars!

We Have Our Athletics Carnival
On The Last Week Of School Every Year

It is a great way to get out all your energy in the last week of school and a great way to catch upp with friends as it is a social event as well as a compeditive event where we show good sportsmanship and resilience. This event is fun and good exersise. Most of our school students advance in sports and express their love for sport by applying for district trials with smiles on the faces and trying their best at getting in to it. All of our students like this event more than others because it is a time where you can see and cheer on your friends and even go up against them which is quite fun. Our whole school would choose to do this instead of any other event that has taken place at this school!

Historic Places
This Is The Statue Of The King Who Founded This School

This statue is placed just behind the school gates. It can be seen from all over the school. Most students find it a quite calming spot to read and study. We are even starting a history lesson about that statue and who it is.

This Was The First Building Bulit In The School

It is now known to the students as the Mcgillan library but origainaly it was a classroom that held all the students of the school. It is named after the teacher who taught at this school first Mr Mcgillan. He spent all his time at this school teaching students of all grades but sadly past. but we honour him with naming the building he taught at after him.
